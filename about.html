Who am I?

I'm a student at Kansas State University majoring in Computer Science with a minor in Political Science. I am on track to graduate in May 2022 and am seeking employment in the computer science field.

What am I passionate about?

Ever since middle school, when I participated in a robotics team, I've loved software and programming. Since then, I've been fortunate to be able to pursue that passion, including by attending K-State! Programming excites me because it draws on my logic and detail skills to solve difficult problems that can affect anywhere from one person to billions of people.

A twin passion has grown up alongside programming, however: reducing political polarization in the United States by promoting the practice of assuming good faith. This desire has informed my engagement in campus politics throughout my time at K-State and I would love to pursue it alongside programming as I move beyond my education.

What are my previous work experiences?

After my junior year of high school, I worked for a small Kansas City-area startup named Helmet Tracker. There, I collected helmet images and contact information for over 700 colleges and universities to enable a national helmet contest called Helmet Bowl. I also collaborated with the Helmet Tracker team to design and implement the contest's voting system in WordPress, which handled over 250,000 votes across all conferences during the first Helmet Bowl.

After my freshman year of college, I worked for RSA Security on the RSA Archer Suite at their Overland Park office. During my internship, I worked on the server side of the Archer Suite implementing REST API endpoints in C#. Those endpoints enabled the creation of an entirely new navigation menu for the application, which was implemented in RSA Archer version 6.7 and is currently used by over 1,300 organizations.

After my sophomore year of college, I worked for Garmin International at their main office in Olathe, Kansas. As a Software Engineer Intern, I worked on flyGarmin, Garmin's web-based portal for pilot avionic databases, registration, and product support. During my time at Garmin, I repaired unit tests in Scala for use in continuous integration and wrote Angular services, components, and unit tests in TypeScript to connect with new flyGarmin APIs. While not all of my changes have shipped to customers, they provided the foundation for improved development practices and a next-generation version of flyGarmin.

What am I involved in at K-State?

In April, I began my third term as a Student Senator for K-State's Student Governing Association (SGA). SGA Student Senate allocates almost $16 million of privilege fee funding between different organizations on campus, including the Union, Recreational Services, Lafene Health Center, and more. SGA also allots a piece of that money to fund travel and on-campus events for registered student organizations and advocates for topics of interest to students to administration and at all levels of government. SGA has been a great way for me to use the Speech and Debate skills I developed during high school to serve students.

I've also consistently participated in the Mobile Development Club at Kansas State University and am currently its president. In this role, I lead Android application development tutorials for a small group of students as well as handle administrative tasks for the club.
